Model UN

Model UN constitute a big part of my life. It started in the pandemic when my friend invited to my first MUN conference. I remember how shy, ineloquant & hesitant I was. Yet, the idea of representing a country and speaking on behalf of a whole nation somehow impressioned me. Thus, I attended more and more MUN conferences online during the pandemic. Each conference brought new people, new ideas, and new perspectives for me. The more I participated in simulations, the more I became self-confident in my speeches, the more I learned how to convince my fellow delegates, the better I wrote positions and resolutions papers and the more awards I started to win. As the pandemic was ending, I wanted to make sure that my story with MUN would not end. I gathered a team of 7 persons who participated in previous MUN conferences and we founded the first MUN club in our region: TIMUN Ezzahra.While more than 100 participants were waiting for my speech during the opening ceremony of our grand simulation held this summer, I realized how much MUN changed me. It did not just teach me how to tie a tie, it helped me develop as a leader and as a person. It opened my eyes to the global problems our world is facing: the unethical use of technology, the environmental threats the earth is suffering from, and the devastating consequences of wars. I know more about the threat of a nuclear attack than any eighteen-year-old should know. On the other hand, it taught me to change the way I look at these problems and always look for solutions. It helped me shape our club the way MUN shaped me; as a leader who is motivated to create a positive change.
